## Setting

The 'verse — a scattered network of colonized star systems, centuries after humanity left Earth. No single government rules everything. Trade routes are contested, corporations are more powerful than governments, and most people are just trying to survive between pay cycles.

You play as a freelance trader/bounty hunter operating out of a beat-up ship with a good coffee maker and a bad attitude.

**Tone:** Gritty, jazzy, lived-in. Think Cowboy Bebop meets Firefly. Nothing is shiny. Everything has history.

## Factions

### The Alliance
The dominant interplanetary government. Controls the core systems. Enforces order through bureaucracy and warships. Not evil, just large — which amounts to the same thing on the outer rim.
- **Market influence:** Alliance Bonds (ALB) rise when they're winning, crash during rebellions.
- **Reference:** Firefly's "Alliance"

### Blue Sun Corporation
Nobody knows what Blue Sun doesn't make. Food, medicine, weapons, information. Their logo appears on everything. Rumor is they own half the Alliance anyway.
- **Market influence:** BSC stock moves on tech news, acquisitions, and "incidents."
- **Reference:** Firefly's Blue Sun Corp.

### The Syndicate
Loose network of crime bosses, smuggling rings, and bounty brokers. Not a single organization — more like a shared understanding between dangerous people.
- **Market influence:** Cortex Credits (CXC) volume spikes when the Syndicate is active.

### Rim Freeholders
Independent colonies and stations on the outer edge of charted space. No loyalty to the Alliance. Trade in anything, ask nothing. Hard people who chose the hard life.
- **Market influence:** Helium-3 and grain prices fluctuate with Rim conflict/stability.

### The Bebop Guild
Bounty hunters operating under a loose code of conduct. If you're registered with the Guild, you can take contracts. If you're not — you're competition.
- **Reference:** Cowboy Bebop's bounty hunter culture.

## Economy Events

Real-world market events translate into in-game narrative moments:

| Real Event | In-Game Event | Narrative |
|---|---|---|
| Stock market crash (>5%) | "Black Thursday on the Cortex" | Alliance credits frozen, trade routes disrupted |
| Crypto bull run | "Cortex Credits surge" | Smugglers flush with cash, Syndicate expands |
| Oil/gas spike | "Helium-3 shortage" | Rim stations ration fuel, ships grounded |
| Gold rally | "Dylinium rush" | Mining claims contested, border skirmishes |
| Recession signal | "Alliance austerity decree" | Bond yields rise, social unrest on core planets |
| Commodity crash | "Rim harvest glut" | Grain cheap, but stations already starving |
